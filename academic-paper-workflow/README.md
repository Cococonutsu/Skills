# AI Conference Paper Workflow

This folder groups three complementary skills for writing and analyzing AI conference papers, especially ML, NLP, CV, multimodal learning, and LLM papers. The focus is not generic academic writing, but top-conference-style paper construction: clear problem framing, reviewer-facing contributions, strong section logic, evidence-backed claims, and precise English prose.

- `research-paper-writing`: paper-level story, section logic, paragraph flow, contribution framing, claim-evidence alignment, and reviewer-facing presentation for AI conference papers.
- `academic-sentence-craft`: sentence-level English polishing for conference-style prose, including subject choice, verb strength, clause order, information focus, result interpretation, and non-native phrasing.
- `paper-reading-analysis`: paper reading and analysis for AI research papers, including motivation, research gap, contributions, methods, experiments, evidence, limitations, and reusable writing patterns.

A typical workflow is: read strong papers first, extract how they frame the problem and evidence, refine the paper-level argument next, and polish individual sentences last.
